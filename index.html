Margaret Hamilton, the first Software Engineer

Margaret Hamilton, a pioneering software engineer, made invaluable contributions to the field of computer science during the 1960s and 1970s. Most notably, she led the team that developed the onboard flight software for NASA's Apollo missions, including the historic Apollo 11 moon landing. Hamilton's groundbreaking work in software engineering laid the foundation for modern software development practices, introducing concepts like software reliability and error recovery. Her dedication to ensuring the safety and reliability of software in critical systems has had a lasting impact on the aerospace industry and beyond, cementing her legacy as a trailblazing figure in the history of computing.

Hamilton is widely credited as being the first person to use the term "software engineering".
